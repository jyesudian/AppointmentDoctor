Avodah User & Administrative Guide

Official Product Handbook for NGO Administrators & Volunteer Clinicians

Last updated: June 2026 • Platform Version 1.1.0

1. Avodah Product Overview

Avodah is a Hebrew word signifying that the work you do with your God-given talents and your service to others are considered acts of worship. The platform's foundation is built upon the biblical reference 1 Peter 4:10: "Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God’s grace in its various forms."

Avodah is a secure, automated coordinate-matching framework designed to manage and coordinate remote community healthcare camp campaigns. The platform acts as a bridge between NGO administrators and volunteer clinicians (doctors and nurses). By registering, maintaining profiles, and uploading license credentials, medical professionals can be verified and deployed efficiently to rural field sectors based on clinical needs.

🛡️ Core Platform Guardrails
  • Verified-Only Matching: Only doctors and nurses whose applications are audited and marked as "Approved" appear in matching pools.
  • Hard Date Gates: The platform checks volunteer planner entries. If a clinician is not explicitly marked available for the campaign's exact date, they are locked out.
  • Execution Date Lock: On-site check-in and check-out tracking controls remain disabled until the scheduled day of the camp campaign.

2. Administrative Portal User Guide

NGO operators access the Avodah Administrative Control Center to monitor network activity, verify volunteer practitioners, map coordinate locations, launch camp campaigns, and run matching engines.

2.1 Overview Dashboard

The primary diagnostic screen allows admins to monitor network metrics at a glance:

  • KPI Panels: Tracks Approved Doctors, Enlisted Nurses, active Field Location Nodes, and Pending Verification applications.
  • Active Camp Campaigns: Renders a catalog of all camps. Scheduled campaigns display live RSVP metrics (Attending/Pending). Drafting campaigns hide RSVP stats and show a Drafting - No Communication label.
  • RSVP Detail View: Clicking Details & RSVP Log ↗ opens a modal containing the list of specialists invited and their current response status.

2.2 Volunteer Verification Registry

Avodah holds all registering practitioners in a Pending state until credentials are professionally audited:

  1. Navigate to the Verification Roster tab.
  2. Select a practitioner and click Audit Credentials. A secure modal will retrieve authenticated degree and license scans.
  3. Review credentials. Click Approve Volunteer to activate them.
  4. If details are incomplete, click Decline Application and enter feedback (e.g., "License document scan is blurry"). The doctor will see this warning on their dashboard to upload a new scan.

2.3 Field Location Registry

Under the Field Locations tab, manage the coordinates where outreach campaigns are conducted. You can register new locations by inputting a Location Name, average distance index (relative to coordinates center), active disease caseload metrics (to measure priority), and GPS coordinates.

2.4 Campaign Configuration & Activation

Use the Configure Camp tab to create new camp deployments:

  • Parameters: Campaign Name, Target Location Node, Date, expected patient capacity, and required specialty list.
  • State Controls: New camps default to Drafting. You can transition them to Scheduled or back to Drafting by clicking Edit Details ✏️.
  • Cancellation: If a camp must be canceled, select the camp, click Cancel Camp 🗑️, and enter a reason. Confirmed clinicians will receive simulated cancellation emails/alerts, and the campaign is deleted.

2.5 AI Match Engine & Invitations

The match engine uses multi-factor weighting factors to rank approved, available clinicians. Select a Scheduled camp, click check-boxes for candidates, select a delivery channel (Web App, Email, or Both), and dispatch invitations.

KPI Balancing Metric Weight Evaluation Rule
Calendar Availability Gate Mandatory Check Clinician must have explicitly toggled "Available" for the campaign's exact month and day. Failing this gates them out completely.
Specialty Alignment 40% Awarded 40 points if the clinician's specialty aligns with the required specialty fields defined in the camp parameters.
Location Priority Ranking 40% Evaluates the volunteer's location priorities rank (Index 0 gets 40 points, Index 1 gets 30, Index 2 gets 20, Index >2 gets 10).
Service Completion History 10% Encourages loyalty. Calculated as: Math.min(completed_days * 2, 10).
Commute Index 10% Calculated from location distance index. (Distance <= 20km: 10 pts; <= 100km: 8 pts; <= 200km: 5 pts; else: 2 pts).

2.6 Day-of Camp Check-in Manager

The on-site execution terminal records real-time clinic logs:

  • Select the camp campaign. The terminal will pull up the list of specialists who accepted invitations.
  • On the campaign execution date, administrators can click Check In 🛫 when volunteers arrive, and Check Out 🛬 when their shift finishes, saving exact timestamps to the system logs.

3. Volunteer Practitioner Portal User Guide

Volunteer clinicians (doctors and nurses) use their portal to manage verification statuses, schedule availability slots, set priority nodes, and accept/decline camp assignments.

3.1 Onboarding & Verification

  1. Click Volunteer Portal on the main header. Select Enlist / Register as New Volunteer.
  2. Complete the fields, specify your primary specialty (e.g., Pediatrics, Gynecology), and enter your medical registration number.
  3. Upload files of your degree certificate and active license copy.
  4. Upon submission, your account is marked Pending Audit. You can log in, but matching invitation engines remain locked until approval.

3.2 Planner Preferences & Scheduler

Once approved, configure your settings to attract invitations:

  • Location Priorities: Drag and order the list of rural zones to prioritize the areas you prefer to travel to.
  • Calendar Planner: Toggle dates on the planner view. The AI Match Engine reads these entries directly to check your availability.

3.3 Mission Invitation Center

When matched and recruited by an administrator, the campaign details appear in your inbox:

  • Review campaign metrics: name, date, clinical specialties, and expected patient counts.
  • Click Accept Invitation ✓ to assign yourself to the campaign, or Decline Invitation ✗.
  • If accepted, the campaign appears under your My Upcoming Missions roster.

3.4 Personal Dashboard

  • My Upcoming Missions: Active upcoming campaigns you have committed to attend.
  • My Completed Campaigns: Historical campaigns where administrators registered your attendance logs.
  • Commitment Summary: Shows cumulative metrics of your completed service days.

4. Exporting this Guide to PDF

This HTML document is optimized for A4-sized print formatting. To save it as a PDF directly:

  1. Click the floating Save / Print as PDF button at the bottom-right of your screen.
  2. In your browser's print dialog box, change the Destination to Save as PDF.
  3. Ensure Layout is set to Portrait.
  4. In **More settings**, check the box for Background graphics (this preserves table styling and header colors).
  5. Click **Save** and select a folder on your computer.
